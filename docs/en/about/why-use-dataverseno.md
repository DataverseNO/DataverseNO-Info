# Why use DataverseNO?

Below are some of the key reasons to use DataverseNO to archive, publish, and reuse data.

## A certified repository

DataverseNO is managed and developed in line with best practices in research data management. It was [CoreTrustSeal](https://www.coretrustseal.org/) certified in 2020 and is currently renewing its certification. CoreTrustSeal is a seal of quality for trustworthy and sustainable repositories.

## Curation and preservation

All datasets deposited in DataverseNO are curated by qualified research data management staff at the partner institutions prior to publication. Curators review each dataset and advise users on how to best align with the [DataverseNO deposit guidelines](../deposit/index.md). The guidelines are based on the [FAIR Principles](https://www.go-fair.org/fair-principles/) to support **F**indable, **A**ccessible, **I**nteroperable, and **R**eusable data, and cover aspects such as metadata and documentation, file organisation and formats.

The curation process distinguishes DataverseNO from many other digital repositories where deposited data are not reviewed before publication. However, curation is not an assessment of your research — it is a support service to help align your data with recognised principles and practices for data archiving, publishing, and preservation.

In addition to curation, DataverseNO ensures long-term preservation of published datasets. Datasets are responsibly maintained for at least 10 years and migrated to new formats if needed.

## Credit and visibility

Using DataverseNO helps you comply with journal and funder requirements, and ensures that others can easily find, access, and credit your dataset:

- **Facilitating peer review:** DataverseNO allows you to share dataset drafts with journal editors and reviewers through a preview URL before publication. For double-blind peer review, curators can create an anonymised version of the dataset draft.
- **Fulfilling data sharing requirements:** Funders, journals, and institutions often require research data to be shared in line with recognised principles. Publishing your data in DataverseNO helps ensure compliance with requirements from [The Research Council of Norway](https://www.forskningsradet.no/), the [European Research Council](https://erc.europa.eu/), and others.
- **Citation:** Datasets in DataverseNO are assigned persistent identifiers (DOIs), providing stable and unique references that enable reliable citation and increase academic credit.
- **Indexing in discovery services:** DataverseNO datasets are indexed in multiple services including [B2FIND](https://b2find.eudat.eu/), [BASE](https://www.base-search.net/), [DataCite Commons](https://commons.datacite.org/), [Google Dataset Search](https://datasetsearch.research.google.com/), and [Oria](https://www.oria.no/).

## Free and supported

DataverseNO is free to use for researchers at Norwegian institutions. Dedicated support staff are available to help you through the deposit and curation process. [Contact your local support](https://site.uit.no/dataverseno/contact/).
