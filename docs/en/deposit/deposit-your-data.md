# Deposit Your Data

## Step 1: Create a user account

Go to [dataverse.no](https://dataverse.no) and log in:

- **Norwegian institution (Feide):** Log in with your institutional credentials
- **International researcher (eduGAIN or ORCID):** Use eduGAIN or ORCID

!!! info "Deposit access"
    Only researchers from Norwegian research organisations and their international collaborators can deposit data. If you are unsure whether you qualify, contact [support@dataverse.no](mailto:support@dataverse.no).

## Step 2: Deposit your data

1. Navigate to your institution's collection in DataverseNO
2. Click **Add Data → New Dataset**
3. Fill in the required metadata fields:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Title | Descriptive — not the same as your article title |
| Author | All contributors; use ORCID lookup where available; names inverted (Family, Given) |
| Contact | Email address for dataset inquiries |
| Description | What the data contains — not a copy of your abstract |
| Subject | Research area (select from dropdown) |
| Keyword | Enter each keyword in a separate field |
| Related publication | Link to your article, or note "Submitted for review" |
| License | **CC0** recommended for maximum reuse |

4. Upload your data files and README. Container files must use `.zip` or `.tar` — no compression passwords or encryption.
5. For large folder structures, folder upload is supported to preserve directory hierarchy.

### Embargo

Files may be restricted for up to two years if there is a good reason (e.g. patent pending). Metadata and README files must always remain public. Do not use embargo simply because a manuscript is under review.

## Step 3: Submit for review

Click **Submit for Review**. Your dataset goes to a curator at your institutional collection for a quality check before the DOI is activated.

The curator may return the dataset to you with feedback — this is a normal part of the process.

## Step 4: Refer to your data

Once published, cite your dataset using the DOI. See [Refer to your data](refer-to-your-data.md).

## Step 5: Modify your data (versioning)

You can update metadata or files after publication. This creates a new version requiring curator approval. See [Modify your data](modify-your-data.md).
